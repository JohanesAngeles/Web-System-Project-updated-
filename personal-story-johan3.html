Returning to Baguio City in April after a hiatus of several years felt like a reunion with an old friend. My initial encounter with this charming city transpired back in 2011, and its allure had lingered in my memory ever since. The temperate climate, scenic vistas, and vibrant cultural tapestry had left an indelible mark, prompting a longing to revisit those cherished moments. Although work commitments necessitated my presence, I embraced the opportunity to rediscover Baguio's essence and embrace the role of a curious explorer.

Arriving in Baguio, I was immediately enveloped in a sense of serenity. The crisp mountain air carried the invigorating scent of pine, while the towering peaks formed a breathtaking backdrop against the sky. Despite the passage of time, the city retained its timeless charm, evoking a comforting sense of familiarity. Interactions with the locals further endeared me to Baguio. Their genuine warmth and hospitality reflected the heart and soul of the city's culture, fostering a sense of kinship and belonging.

Each day in Baguio unfolded as a journey of discovery. I ventured to the renowned La Trinidad Strawberry Farm, where the sight of lush strawberry fields delighted the senses and offered a tantalizing taste of local produce. Wandering through the verdant expanses of Burnham Park, I reveled in the lively atmosphere, as families picnicked by the lake and children frolicked amidst the vibrant blooms. At Mines View, I stood in awe of the panoramic vistas stretching out before me, a testament to the awe-inspiring beauty of nature.

Exploring the bustling thoroughfares of Session Road, I found myself immersed in the vibrant pulse of the city. The eclectic mix of shops, restaurants, and street vendors offered a glimpse into the rich tapestry of Baguio's cultural heritage. A visit to The Mansion, with its stately architecture and manicured gardens, provided a glimpse into the city's colonial past and left me captivated by its grandeur.
